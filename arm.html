Robotic Arm as an
Assistive Technology


This is a project for Dean's Undergraduate Summer Research at NYU Shanghai. We are trying to explore the possility to use a robotic arm as an assistive technology for disabled people.

The first part of this project is to use head movement to control the robotics arm rotation. We used a fix camera to track the movement of someone's head, and then map the movement to robotic arm.

And the second part of this project that we worked on is the arm automatically detect an object and grab it. A camera is fixed to the top of the arm. We used OpenCV to detect the position of an object. Then the arm moves to center the object and strectches to grab the object. But this part didn't work out very well by the end of the summer. Our arm structure had a very limited degree of freedom in terms of stretching.

We used Arduino, OpenCV and a software HeadMouse for this project.
