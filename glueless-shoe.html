Glueless Shoe


I tested it! It's really comfortable and strong.





Intro & Research


I took a shoemaking class at risd. This is the first shoe that I designed and made.



When I made this shoe, I got a slightly headache everytime I used the shoe glue, contact cement. Making this shoe makes me reflect on the process of shoemaking.

Contact cement is the most common glue used in the shoe industry nowadays. It's incredibly strong, but super toxic. It's very not healthy for the shoe factory workers and not good for the wearers in the long term.

Besides that, because the shoe parts are permentantly glued together, it's difficult to replace and repair parts when one part run out. According to the U.S. Department of the Interior, Americans throw away at least 300 million pairs of shoes each year. Chemicals and fossil fuels are produced and leaked into the environment during the first and last steps in the shoe life cycle.

This research brought me to the idea of designing a pair of glueless shoes that are comfortable to wear, durable and easy to repair and replace parts.




Making Process


This project was lots of experiments to figure out how to make the shoe sole.

I first modeled and 3d printed a plastic shoe sole based on the size of my shoe last. Then put some clay on the sole to carve out details. After getting the cast piece, I poured silicone rubber on it to get the two-piece mold. I chose urethane rubber (smoothon pmc-770) as the sole material, which has an ideal hardness of shoes. After casted a few shoe soles, I designed a pattern for the shoe. In the end I sewed the shoe upper and use threads to put the upper and sole together.
